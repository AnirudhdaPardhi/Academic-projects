Introduction¶

The objective of this project is to predict the probability of cervical cancer using classification machine learning methods. The dataset was sourced from the UCI Machine Learning Repository at https://archive.ics.uci.edu/ml/datasets/Cervical+cancer+%28Risk+Factors%29# . This report is organized as follows:

  1. Overview section describes the dataset used and the features in this dataset.
  2. Data Preparation section covers data cleaning and data preparation steps
  3. Data Exploration and visualisation section explores significant features and their inter-relationships using visualisation techniques.
  4. Summary and Conclusions section describes the major important impofmation extraced from dataset.

Overview¶

Data Source¶

Data was downloaded in csv format from UCI Machine Learning Repository.The dataset has 858 observations. Characteristics of Dataset is Multivariate. Datasets consist of 36 number of attributes which contains continuous and categorical datatype attributes. In this project, we will split training and test dataset within this dataset.

Project Objective¶

Our goal focuses on the prediction of indicators/diagnosis of cervical cancer.There are 4 target variables in the dataset which as combination will gives us level of risk in predicting cancer successfully.

Target Feature¶

Our target features are Hinselmann, Schiller, Citology and Biopsy which are categorical features. Hence, our project is based on a classification problem.

Descriptive Features¶

The variable descriptions are as below :

  • (int) Age
  • (int) Number of sexual partners

  • (bool) STDs
  • (int) STDs (number)
  • (bool) STDs:condylomatosis
  • (bool) STDs:cervical condylomatosis
  • (bool) STDs:vaginal condylomatosis
  • (bool) STDs:vulvo-perineal condylomatosis
  • (bool) STDs:syphilis
  • (bool) STDs:pelvic inflammatory disease
  • (bool) STDs:genital herpes
  • (bool) STDs:molluscum contagiosum
  • (bool) STDs:AIDS

  • (int) STDs: Number of diagnosis

  • List of numerical continuous attributes
  • List of categorical attributes

Summary and Conclusions¶

From the above illustrated single variable histograms and boxplot it is evident that the mean age of the women facing the risk of cancer is 26.8 years. From density curve graphs we can say that women start their first sexual intercourse around the age of 15 years. As the age of women increases their respective number of sexual partners tends to increase too which further increases the possibility of getting cancer. Based on the Scatterplot for First sexual intercourse vs Number of pregnancies we can depict this fact that higher chances of pregnancy among women are between age of 15 to 20. Based on the boxplot illustration we can conclude that patients who have cancer are in higher age group as compare to non-cancer patient. As per Scatterplot for First sexual intercourse vs Number of pregnancies by HIV disease status we can depict this fact that HIV status of women are more positive who had early first sexual intercourse i.e between age of 15 to 20. As per Boxplot of Smoking status vs Age by Cancer status those who smokes and have cancer falls in comparatively higher age groups than Non-smoker.
